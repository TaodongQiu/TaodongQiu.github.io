TIMELINE

History and Development of Cyberpunk.

1968

Philip K. Dick novel Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep was published and generally cited as proto-cyberpunk.

1982

The first Cyberpunk movie—Blade Runner—was released.

1984

Regarded as ground-breaking and sometimes as "the archetypal cyberpunk work", William Gibson's novel Neuromancer was published.

1988

Katsuhiro Otomo's manga series Akira was adapted into an animated film adaptation, which later popularizing the subgenre and inspired a wave of Japanese cyberpunk works.

1995

Ghost in the Shell (1989) influenced a number of prominent filmmakers. Its 1995 anime film adaptation inspired The Wachowskis to create The Matrix (1999) and its sequels.

1999

The Matrix was released and has since appeared in lists of the greatest science fiction films.

2017

As a sequel to the 1982 film Blade Runner, Blade Runner 2049 received five nominations at the 90th Academy Awards, winning Best Cinematography and Best Visual Effects.

2017

Bloober Team's psychological horror video game >Observer_ was released and received generally favourable reviews from critics. They praised the world design, visual effects and hacking sequences but criticised the stealth areas and its focus on style over substance.

Games

Most Impressive and Representative Cyberpunk Games.

>observer_

Bloober Team

The player controls Daniel Lazarski, a Krakówian detective of the Observers police unit. He can hack people's brain implants with a device known as the Dream Eater, for interrogation purposes. Equipped with augmented vision split into Electromagnetic Vision—which scans for electronic devices—and Bio Vision—which scans for biological evidence—he is able to analyse and highlight certain objects in his environment.

Detroit: Become Human

Quantic Dream

The game revolves around three androids: Kara (Valorie Curry), who escapes the owner she was serving to explore her newfound sentience and protect a young girl; Connor (Bryan Dechart), whose job is to hunt down sentient androids; and Markus (Jesse Williams), who devotes himself to releasing other androids from servitude. They may survive or perish depending on dialogue choices that shape the story.

Cyberpunk 2077

CD Projekt

Adapted from the 1988 tabletop game Cyberpunk 2020, it is set fifty-seven years later in dystopian Night City, California, an open world with six distinct regions. In a first-person perspective, players assume the role of the customisable mercenary V, who can apply experience points to stat upgrades to reach prominence in the character classes NetRunner, Techie, and Solo.
